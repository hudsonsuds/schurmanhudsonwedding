Do I need a car?

Well, that depends on where you’re coming from:

  • If you fly into Monterey Airport, and only plan to be in the area for the wedding weekend, a rental car isn’t required. It will be just a 25-35 minute cab ride to most of our suggested accommodations.
  • Although not the quickest option around the area, Monterey and Carmel do have public buses that run on the hour. The 24 lines runs up and down Carmel Valley Road, and bring visitors to and from downtown Carmel and Monterey. You can also swap buses, and head all the way down to Big Sur on the 22. Bus information here.
  • What about to and from the wedding itself? We will have shuttle services set-up from Holman Ranch to Carmel Valley Ranch and to the Hyatt. If there is another location that fills with addition family and friends, we may make an extra shuttle stop there too! Please let us know where you plan to stay, when you decide.
  • Rental cars in San Francisco or Monterey will be anywhere from $30-75 a day depending on when you book it, and what type you choose.

Make it a Vacation

Lake Tahoe

The best time to visit Lake Tahoe (if you love hiking, biking or relaxing lakeside) is from September to November (as temperatures drop, from the low 70s to the high 40s, so do the hotel costs). The drive is amazingly beautiful in itself, and just 5.5 hours from Carmel Valley.

Yosemite National Park

Yosemite is one of the most breathtaking places that California has to offer. The park (747,956 acres) reaches across the western slopes of the Sierra Nevada mountain chain, and is 4 hours northeast of Carmel Valley. Yosemite is internationally recognized for its spectacular granite cliffs, waterfalls, clear streams, Giant Sequoia groves, and biological diversity. Julia and Matt loved staying at the Evergreen Lodge, but there many options!

Northern Wine Country

Napa and the surrounding wine country (Sonoma, Healdsburg) is only a 3.5 hour drive to Carmel Valley. Make hotel reservations sooner rather than later as October is harvest season, Wine Countries busiest time. June through October offers the warmest, sunniest and driest California weather, so it's no surprise this is "high season" for California wine country. Looking for the best spot for a picnic and wine overlooking the rolling hills of Napa? Julia & Matt’s recommendation is William Hill Winery!

Marin

There is so much to do right outside if San Francisco itself, like explore Marin! Marin is filled to the brim with beautiful hikes, gorgeous beaches, redwood forests, yummy restaurants, and amazing overlooks. Popular hot spots are Stinson Beach, Muir Woods, Muir Beach, Mount Tamalpais, Tiburon (most specifically, Sam’s Anchor Cafe brunch), Angel Island, and Sausalito. Matt and Julia got engaged on the Steep Ravine Trail above Stinson Beach and lunched at Trident Restaurant overlooking the bay after - two of their favorite places!

San Francisco

San Francisco is just 2.25 hours north of Carmel Valley. The list of things to do in San Francisco is much too long. October is San Francisco’s Indian Summer and an extremely popular time to be in the city. Book your hotel rooms sooner rather than later. FYI -- There is a city-wide medical conference in SF Monday-Wednesday that week, you will find better rates before or after those dates.

Carmel (by-the-sea) and Monterey

With plenty to do - shopping, golfing (the famous Pebble Beach Golf Course), scenic drives (try 17 mile drive!), a great aquarium, historical sites and national parks, the area can keep you quite busy. During October, weather can range along the coast with a chilly foggy day to a warm, sunny afternoon.

Carmel Valley

Carmel Valley is set inland from Carmel and Monterey’s coast, lending to warmer weather and sunnier days. The valley is more about hiking, wine tasting, eating or relaxing by a pool than much else. If you plan to call the Valley homebase for a couple of days, it would be wise to have a car for exploring or be content with laying low at your lodging.

Big Sur

Jagged cliffs pummeled by surf line the Pacific Coast Highway along Big Sur, creating some of California's most memorable ocean vistas between Carmel and the northern tip of San Luis Obispo County. Hike, camp, explore the coastline, or enjoy the romantic lodging and relaxing spas. Suggestions: Post Ranch Inn, Ventana Inn and Spa, Deetjens Inn, Fernwood Resort, Ragged Point Inn.

Hearst Castle

Just 2.5 hours south of Carmel Valley is Hearst Castle. Reserve tickets online up to 56 days in advance to tour Hearst Castle (you must reserve tour tickets early, they sell out quickly), a museum unlike any other. From experiencing what life was like as a guest of W. R. Hearst to exploring his personal collection of rare and ancient works of art, encounter the majesty and beauty of this historical treasure first-hand with a guided Hearst Castle tour.
